About the Builder
I'm Jeremy, a part‑time open‑source developer, father of seven, and long‑time Bitcoin Cash supporter. I've been building MintCore and CashMint Studio because I want BCH to be accessible to creators, developers, and new people entering the ecosystem.

Over the past year, I've been developing MintCore from the ground up as a clean, well‑tested CashTokens SDK designed for real‑world apps. Alongside it, I've been building CashMint Studio, a creator‑first minting platform focused on making token creation simple, safe, and approachable for beginners.

I'm committed to delivering a stable, documented, production‑ready stack that the BCH community can rely on, and I'm prepared to take this work full‑time if the funding and ecosystem support make it possible.

I've felt the friction firsthand, and I want to make things clearer and easier for the next person who tries to build something on BCH.

Why this matters
BCH has powerful technology, but too many people hit walls when they try to build with it. Developers run into scattered examples, outdated libraries, and unclear patterns. Creators want to mint tokens but don't know where to start or which tools are safe. Newcomers often feel like they need to be experts just to participate.

I've seen these challenges firsthand while building MintCore and CashMint Studio. The potential is there, but the path isn't clear enough — and that slows down adoption, creativity, and real‑world use.

MintCore gives developers a clean, reliable foundation for CashTokens. CashMint Studio gives creators a simple, safe way to mint without needing to understand the underlying complexity. Together, they lower the barrier for anyone who wants to build on BCH, whether they're writing code, launching a project, or just exploring what's possible.

BCH grows when people can build without friction. Clear tools, good documentation, and accessible platforms make the ecosystem stronger for everyone.

What this Flipstarter makes possible
Building on BCH has shown me where the friction points are — missing patterns, unclear examples, tools that feel unfinished, and creator experiences that aren't as welcoming as they should be. The work funded here is focused on removing those barriers so developers, creators, and newcomers can build with confidence.

To make that progress clear, I've broken the work into milestones that reflect the real steps needed to bring MintCore and CashMint Studio to a polished, production‑ready state.

Milestones

MintCore v1 Release
Repo: https://github.com/mintcoredev/MintCore
I've run into missing patterns, unclear examples, and inconsistent tooling while building on BCH, and MintCore is my answer to those frustrations. This milestone delivers the first stable, well‑tested release of MintCore, including finalized APIs, improved error handling, consistent transaction‑building patterns, and a clean developer experience. The goal is to give builders a dependable SDK they can use without reverse‑engineering or guesswork.

CashMint Studio: Creator‑Friendly Minting Flow
I've seen how overwhelming token creation can feel for newcomers, and I want the Studio to remove that anxiety. This milestone focuses on completing the full minting flow: intuitive UI steps, safe defaults, clear validation, and a guided experience that helps creators mint confidently without needing to understand the underlying complexity of CashTokens.

Documentation and Examples
I've spent a lot of time piecing together information that should have been documented clearly from the start, and I don't want others to go through that. This milestone delivers thorough documentation for both MintCore and CashMint Studio, including examples, walkthroughs, and practical guides that help developers and creators get started quickly and safely.

Accessibility and UX Improvements
I want people to feel comfortable using BCH tools even if they're not technical, and accessibility plays a big part in that. This milestone improves layout, readability, keyboard navigation, mobile responsiveness, and overall clarity so the Studio feels welcoming and easy to use for everyone.

Testing, Stability, and Final Polish
I've learned firsthand how important reliability is when building on BCH, especially when real value is involved. This milestone focuses on strengthening test coverage, improving stability, tightening edge cases, and ensuring both MintCore and the Studio are ready for real‑world use. The goal is to ship tools the community can trust.

Budget and Funding
This Flipstarter supports focused development time, testing, documentation, UX improvements, and the final polish needed to deliver stable, production‑ready releases of both MintCore and CashMint Studio. The funding allows me to dedicate consistent time to this work and move these tools from strong foundations to fully reliable, community‑ready products.

Closing
I believe BCH becomes stronger when builders and creators have tools that are clear, accessible, and trustworthy. That's what this work is about — lowering friction, empowering new voices, and helping the ecosystem grow in a sustainable way.
